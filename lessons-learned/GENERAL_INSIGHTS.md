# General Insights

## 2026-03-25 - Thin wrappers age better than vendored prompt packs

Keeping fast-changing workflow logic upstream and wrapping it locally creates less drift than copying the workflow into the project.

## 2026-03-25 - Review loops matter more than idea generation alone

The strongest value in this workflow is not just producing an idea quickly. It is forcing the claims, implementation, and paper framing through repeated criticism.

## 2026-03-25 - A theorem can feel physics-heavy even when physics is not the main obstacle

In the Gleason project, the cited paper's proof was already mostly geometry and analysis. The real accessibility barrier was not advanced physics vocabulary but the level of abstract reasoning needed to complete the rigidity argument.

## 2026-03-25 - Constructive or effective does not automatically mean beginner-accessible

In the Gleason project, later sources suggested constructive and effective proofs exist. That helped clarify the theorem's proof landscape, but it did not remove the underlying geometric and analytic difficulty for a high school audience.

## 2026-03-25 - Symbolic checks are worth using for proof surgery

In the Gleason project, exact symbolic matrix and intersection checks helped separate real proof gaps from notation bugs and made the zero-circle step much more trustworthy.
